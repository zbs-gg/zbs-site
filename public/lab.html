01Why this

Every new chat starts from zero. You explain again who you are, what you're working on, and what's already decided. Decisions live in calls, the reasons behind them vanish, and summaries blend fact with guess until you no longer know what to trust.

This isn't a bug in one model. It's the absence of a memory layer between you, your projects, your team, and your tools. Context leaks at every seam — between sessions, between people, between tools — and you pay for it in time and repeated mistakes.

The action layer is one thing; the memory layer is another — it holds decisions, their reasons, and continuity. Without it, every system has to be brought up to speed again each morning. This lab is about building that layer and proving it works.

02The hidden problems

You thought it was one problem. It's five.

"The AI forgets" is just the tip. Underneath it are five, and each costs more than the first.

01

A guess passed off as fact

The AI answers smoothly and convincingly — but you can't see where your words end and its invention begins. You decide on a blend of truth and plausibility.

02

A blind handoff

Before it answers, you can't see what it's leaning on. What made it into its "head" and what didn't stays unknown until it's too late.

03

Corrections don't stick

You fix it — and next session the same mistake, as if the conversation never happened. The correction never becomes memory.

04

"Deleted" comes back

You thought you removed it from context, and it resurfaces in answers. Between "forget" and "actually erase" lies a gulf.

05

The reason is lost in a team

"We chose this" survives; "why exactly" does not. A strong person's expertise dies in their chats, and the next hire starts with archaeology.

03Why the usual fixes don't work

How people usually try to solve it

You've probably tried some of these. Here's why none of them closed the problem.

"Just write better prompts"
✕A prompt is a one-shot instruction, not memory. However well you tune it, every session you explain yourself again. It improves one answer and remembers nothing of the next.
"Put everything in a second brain"
✕An archive where everything is filed and nothing is found. It stores documents, not state and decisions. The AI doesn't go there itself and doesn't know what's still current.
"Paste the whole history into context"
✕Noise and cost. The model still blends fact with guess; what you pasted has no source and no way to correct. The more you paste, the less you can trust it.
"RAG over all chats, auto-import"
✕It retrieves the plausibly-wrong, confidently. Nothing is reviewed, private things leak into shared context, and without review and expiry it easily drags up the stale.
"The assistant's built-in memory"
✕Often opaque: hard to inspect, correct, or set an expiry. And it travels poorly between tools — switch models and you start almost from scratch.

What's missing isn't another store. It's a verifiable layer — where every fact has a source, a status (fact or hypothesis), and a lifespan, and you can inspect it before it's passed, correct it, and delete it.

06What you keep

Proof, not a presentation

The lab ends in checks that either pass or don't — live, in front of you.

  1. A fresh session recalls the right reviewed context.
  2. You see exactly what was passed — and what was left out.
  3. A correction changes the next answer.
  4. Deletion removes the record from the working layer; we verify it with a new session.
  5. (Team) a new person or agent reconstructs a decision, its reason, and its status — without the founder retelling it.
