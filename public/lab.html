isn't this just a vector search?

No. The same question needs a different memory depending on the moment.

won't it leak my private stuff to the team?

Every memory carries a visibility. Work is shared. The boss memes are not.

Each entry knows where it came from, whether it's fact or hypothesis, who can see it, and when it expires. Sharing into the team layer is consent-gated — personal stays personal.

how do i know it won't repeat old, wrong answers?

Superseded decisions are marked superseded — not silently re-served.

When a decision changes, the old one isn't deleted and isn't surfaced as current. The new one wins, with a source. No confident answers from last quarter's plan.

faq

Honest answers.

Is this for solo builders?
No — solo "second brain" tools already exist. This is built for teams and companies: shared context across people, where one person's knowledge stops dying in their chat.
Which harnesses does it work with?
It's harness-agnostic — Claude Code, Kilo Code, OpenCode, Codex, ChatGPT, Cursor, Slack. The memory lives across all of them; you continue the work wherever you are.
Does it learn by itself / is it autonomous?
No. It proposes; a human approves or rejects. Nothing changes the shared layer without a yes. The magic is compounding quality at near-zero added effort — not running without you.
How do you measure emotion without it being creepy?
Affective state is a retrieval signal (frustrated, blocked, shipping) and a privacy boundary — venting is flagged private and never shared. It surfaces strengths and context, it doesn't score or rank people.
What about our data?
It runs on your stack. You see what's stored and passed; you can correct, supersede, expire and delete. Team sharing is consent-gated and reviewed — not raw transcripts.
What's the refund?
Week 1 ends with a baseline test on your data. If we can't reproduce the recall failure you brought, we refund — no questions.
